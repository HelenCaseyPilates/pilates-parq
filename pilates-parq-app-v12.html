Duty of Care & Insurance (Ireland)

Under Irish health and safety legislation and the common law duty of care, all fitness professionals and studio operators have a legal obligation to pre-screen clients prior to exercise participation. Completion of a health screening questionnaire such as this one forms part of that duty and supports the studio's public liability and professional indemnity insurance obligations. Fitness professionals in Ireland are required to hold valid Public Liability and Professional Indemnity insurance in line with Coaching Ireland's Code of Ethics and the requirements of most insurance underwriters. In the event that a client does not disclose a relevant health condition and an incident arises, a completed and signed PAR-Q/PAR-Q+ on record may significantly reduce the studio's legal exposure. This form does not replace a full medical assessment; clients who disclose health conditions should be referred to an appropriate GP or allied health professional before commencing or continuing participation.

Data Protection & GDPR Notice

The health information collected in this form constitutes special category personal data under Article 9 of the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Irish Data Protection Act 2018. This data is collected solely for the purpose of assessing a client's readiness to participate in Pilates classes and supporting the studio's duty of care obligations. It will be stored securely with access restricted to authorised studio instructors only. Under GDPR's storage limitation principle, personal data must not be kept for longer than is necessary for its purpose. In line with best practice for health and fitness records in Ireland — and taking into account the two-year limitation period for personal injury claims under the Statute of Limitations (Amendment) Act 1991, and the requirements of General Condition 5 of the studio's professional liability insurance policy (Balens Europe B.V. / Hiscox SA), which mandates retention of records for a minimum of six years from the date of treatment, and the recommended minimum periods issued by the Data Protection Commission (DPC) — completed health screening records will be retained for a minimum of 6 years from the date of last class attendance, and securely deleted thereafter unless a legal claim is pending. Clients have the right to access, rectify, or request erasure of their personal data at any time by contacting the studio directly. For any queries regarding how your data is stored or used, please speak with your instructor. The Data Protection Commission (DPC) may impose fines of up to €20 million or 4% of global annual turnover for non-compliance with GDPR.
